Energy bars (Jan bars)

I call these Jan bars(pronouced "y-uh-n") because when I worked at The Keg, my co-worker, Jan, brought these delicous homemade energy bars that he would always share. I kept pestering him for the recipe until he finally caved. After he wrote the recipe out for me, he dubbed them "Jan bars." He also has a shooter that he named "Yummy Jan's" and everytime he offers that to a guest, I just crack up.

Anyways! I make these bars on a weekly basis, they are an easy, portable option, packed full of whole food goodness. The naturally sweet dates contain essential nutrients, vitamins, and minerals to help keep you healthy and active. With the combination of raw cocoa, protein-rich nuts and shredded coconuts you'll have everything you'll need to keep that sweet tooth in check while keeping your body well fueled.

What you'll need

  • 2 Cups (250g) Cashew nuts
  • 1/2 Cups (70g) Almonds (skins removed)
  • 2 Cups (300g) Medjool Dates, chopped and pitted
  • 3/4 Cup (75g) Cocoa Powder
  • 1/2 Cup (40g) Unsweetened Shredded Coconuts
  • 2 Tbsp Vanilla Extract
  • 1-2 Tbsp Cold Water
  • Pinch of sea salt

Equipment

  • Food processor
  • Baking pan
  • Aluminum Foil

Everything you'll need

Step 1

Combine nuts in food processor and pulse until crumbly but still with abit of texture

Step 2

Add chopped dates, cocoa powder, shredded coconuts, and sea salt blend until combined

Step 3

Then add vanilla extract and water slowly until it becomes a doughy but dry consistency

Step 4

Transfer the mixture onto a baking pan lined with aluminum foil, pressed evenly and chill in the fridge for about an hour before cutting into even sized pieces

Yum!

Method

  1. Combine nuts in food processor and pulse until crumbly but still with abit of texture
  2. Add chopped dates, cocoa powder, shredded coconuts, and sea salt blend until combined
  3. Then add vanilla extract and water slowly until it becomes a doughy but dry consistency
  4. Transfer the mixture onto a baking pan lined with aluminum foil, pressed evenly and chill in the fridge for about an hour before cutting into even sized pieces
